The Beginnings

Spyro the Dragon is a PlayStation game released on September 9, 1998. It was developed by Insomniac Games and published by Sony Computer Entertainment. It's centered on the adventure of a purple dragon named Spyro, in which he has to defeat Gnasty Gnorc and free all dragons. The game focuses on platforming and collecting treasures. This game was notably one of the first to use a 3D panoramic engine. Wikipedia page.

The Sequel

Spyro 2: Ripto's Rage! is the sequel to Spyro the Dragon released on November 2, 1999. Like its predecessor, it was developed by Insomniac Games and published by Sony Computer Entertainment. This game follows Spyro as he is accidentally transported into a new world and must defeat Ripto in order to get home. Wikipedia page.

The Finale of the Trilogy

Spyro: Year of the Dragon is the third and final Spyro game to be developed by Insomniac Games. It was released on October 24, 2000 as a sequel to Spyro 2: Ripto's Rage. This time, Spyro has to retrieve dragon eggs that have been stolen by the evil Sorceress. Wikipedia page.

Legacy

The PlayStation Spyro trilogy remains one of the most popular series released for the system. While there were also titles released for the PlayStation 2, PlayStation 3 and other platforms, none of them were developed by Insomniac Games and failed to garner as much fame among fans as the original trilogy. Notably, the original trilogy has been fully remastered and released as a combined game under the title of Spyro: Reignited Trilogy for the PlayStation 4, Xbox One, Windows and Nintendo Switch.
